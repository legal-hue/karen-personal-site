About

I was born in Accra. At sixteen, I founded an NGO. At twenty-five, I'd worked across three continents. Now I practise law and build things that matter.

I'm a Barrister at Law, trained at Francis Taylor Building and the Government Legal Department. I hold an LLM in Energy, Environment, and Natural Resources Law from SOAS, a Chartered Manager qualification (CMgr, Distinction) from the CMI, and was a Dean's Fellowship Scholar at UCLA. I trained for the Bar at BPP and read Law at the University of Kent.

Before the Bar, I worked in international development and natural resource governance, including at Save The Children International. At sixteen, I founded Youth Development Ghana — raising awareness of child trafficking and slave labour in the cocoa, fishing, and farming industries. I've written for the BBC, The Independent, the Bar Council, the Congressional Black Caucus, and The Voice.

Today I work across law, consultancy, and writing — always at the intersection of justice, development, and the systems that shape who benefits and who doesn't.
