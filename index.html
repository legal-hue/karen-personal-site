About

I was born in Accra. At seventeen, I founded an NGO. At twenty-five, I'd worked across three continents. Now I practise law and build things that matter.

I'm a Barrister at Law, trained at Francis Taylor Building — one of the UK's leading environmental and planning law chambers — and the Government Legal Department. Before the Bar, I spent years in international development and natural resource governance, including at Save The Children International, where I created Mosaic, a diversity network for employees of colour.

I've written with the Congressional Black Caucus, been recognised as one of Britain's Future Leaders and a Future of Ghana 30 Under 30, and received a scholarship from UCLA and the Dean's Fellowship Award. I founded Youth Development Ghana when I was still in school, because I couldn't wait for someone else to do it.

Today I work across law, consultancy, and writing — always at the intersection of justice, development, and the systems that shape who benefits and who doesn't.
